# Water-Puppetry Folk-Tale Repertoire (Tich Tro)

> Reference catalog used by the `score_folktale_repertoire` tool and the
> `sub-core-analysis` skill for heritage grounding (Quality Gate G1). Sourced
> from SECOND-KNOWLEDGE-BRAIN.md Section 1.1.2 and the documented living
> tradition of Vietnamese water puppetry (mu'a ro^'i nu+o+'c).

The traditional water-puppetry repertoire consists of 30+ standardized scenes
(*tich tro*) organized in episodic 3-5 minute segments linked by the Chu Te^u
narrator. Use this catalog to select and ground a scenario before designing
puppet mechanisms, lighting, and effects.

## Scene Structure Convention

1. **Opening** - Chu Te^u introduces the scene with social commentary.
2. **Core action** - puppet mechanism performs the narrative beat (pole/rudder or buoyancy-driven).
3. **Musical cue** - cheo / chau va<n accompaniment marks transitions.
4. **Closing** - narrator recap or direct transition to the next tich tro.

## Heritage Grounding Rule (G1)

A scenario is *grounded* iff its theme matches at least one repertoire entry
above (matched by `score_folktale_repertoire`). If no match, the agent must
either (a) re-source the folk tale from heritage references in
SECOND-KNOWLEDGE-BRAIN.md Section 1.1.2, or (b) flag the scenario as
non-traditional with an explicit limitation notice.
